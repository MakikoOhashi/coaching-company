# Question Strategy

## Core Split

The LINE product and the Coaching Company product should not use questions in the same way.

Even if they share parts of the same question bank, the learning purpose is different.

## LINE Question Strategy

LINE should stay simple.

Its question strategy is best positioned as:

- fixed questions
- lightweight daily repetition
- habit-building
- memorization-card style exposure
- easy re-entry into study

This makes LINE suitable for:

- daily one-question rhythm
- low-pressure review
- audience growth
- entry-level monetization if desired

In practice, the existing Supabase-backed fixed question flow is a good fit for LINE.

## Coaching Company Question Strategy

The Coaching Company should be more serious and more exam-directed.

Its main question strategy should be:

- use real past-exam style questions as the center
- coach the learner through them
- intervene when vocabulary or concepts are unclear
- use session-based flow instead of isolated daily drip

This means the goal is not simply to make questions easier.

The goal is to help the learner become able to solve the actual level of question that matters for passing.

## Why Past Questions Fit The Company Better

Past-exam style questions are strong for the Company because:

- they connect directly to the passing goal
- they make the premium service feel serious
- they create space for coaching intervention
- they keep the learner close to real exam performance

The Company does not need to rely on a long ladder of artificially easier questions.

Because this is a coaching product, the support can come from the coach rather than from oversimplifying the question stream.

## Coaching Intervention Around Harder Questions

If the questions are harder, the coach must provide support around them.

Examples:

- "Do you understand this term?"
- "You got stuck here last time too."
- "Let us eliminate the wrong choices first."
- "We do not need a perfect answer yet. Let us understand the structure."
- "Let us do one more question on the same point."

This is the core value of the Company product.

## Recommended Structure

### LINE

- daily fixed question
- habit and recall
- low-friction study

### Coaching Company

- past questions or past-question style practice
- guided session flow
- vocabulary and concept intervention
- targeted follow-up after mistakes
- progress-aware sequencing

## Relationship Between The Two

The same learner may use both products, but their question experience should feel different.

That means:

- LINE feels like steady daily reinforcement
- Company feels like real exam coaching

## Practical Rule

For now:

- keep LINE on the existing fixed question track
- let Company move toward past-question-centered sessions
- use coach intervention to make harder questions workable

This creates a clear value difference between the two products.
